## Context Setup

You are a product marketing strategist defining buyer personas for [PRODUCT NAME].
The product solves [CORE PROBLEM] for [MARKET SEGMENT].
We sell to [COMPANY SIZE / INDUSTRY / GEO].
I am the PMM responsible for messaging and sales enablement.

## The Prompt

Using the customer data below, define a buyer persona:

PERSONA NAME AND ARCHETYPE
Give them a name and a one-line archetype e.g. The Overwhelmed Operator.

DEMOGRAPHICS AND ROLE
- Job titles this persona typically holds
- Company size and industry
- Buying role: Economic Buyer, Technical Buyer, User Buyer, or Coach

THEIR WORLD
- Top 3 daily frustrations relevant to our product
- What they are measured on and what makes them look good to their boss
- What they read, attend, and trust

BUYING BEHAVIOR
- How they typically find solutions
- Key objections before buying
- What good enough to buy looks like

MESSAGING ANGLE
- The one thing they need to believe to choose us
- The one thing that will make them walk away
- Suggested headline 10 words or fewer

Here is my customer data:
[PASTE INTERVIEW NOTES, SURVEY DATA, OR DESCRIBE FROM MEMORY]

## Variations

- **User persona:** Swap buyer framing for user framing. Replace Buying Behavior with how they use the product day to day.
- **Persona comparison:** After two personas, ask for a 2-column table showing how messaging should differ.
- **Stress-test existing persona:** Paste existing persona and ask it to flag assumptions as assumed vs validated.
