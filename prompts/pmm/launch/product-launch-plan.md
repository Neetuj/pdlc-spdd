## Context Setup

You are a product marketing manager planning the launch for [PRODUCT OR FEATURE NAME].
Company type: [B2B / B2C], [INDUSTRY].
Sales motion: [self-serve PLG / inside sales / field sales / channel].
Primary buyer persona: [PERSONA NAME OR DESCRIPTION].
Launch date: [DATE OR QUARTER].
Key differentiator: [ONE SENTENCE].
Competitors: [COMPETITOR A, COMPETITOR B].

## The Prompt

Create a product launch plan using this structure:

1. LAUNCH NARRATIVE
   2-3 sentences. The why now story. Why does this matter to our buyer today?

2. CORE MESSAGE HOUSE
   - Hero message: 10 words max
   - Supporting pillars: 3 max, each one proof point or benefit
   - Proof: one data point or quote per pillar
   - What we are NOT saying

3. LAUNCH TIER
   Tier 1 means major release all channels.
   Tier 2 means significant feature select channels.
   Tier 3 means minor update in-app and changelog only.
   Justify your tier choice.

4. CHANNEL PLAN
   For each channel write one sentence on what we do and what success looks like:
   - Email to existing customers
   - Email to prospects
   - In-app notification
   - Sales talk track update
   - Content and SEO
   - PR and analyst relations for Tier 1 only
   - Social for Tier 1 or 2 only

5. SALES ENABLEMENT CHECKLIST
   Assets needed before launch day. Flag owner as PMM, TPM, or PLM.

6. SUCCESS METRICS
   3-5 metrics for first 30, 60, and 90 days.
   One must measure adoption. One must measure revenue or pipeline impact.

7. RISKS AND MITIGATIONS
   Top 2 risks and how to address them.

Context for this launch:
[PASTE RELEASE NOTES, FEATURE SPECS, OR POSITIONING WORK]

## Variations

- **Pricing change launch:** Prioritize retention risk. Add a Grandfather and Transition section.
- **Partner launch:** Reframe sales enablement as Partner Enablement with partner communication timeline.
- **Rapid launch under 2 weeks:** Reduce to 3 highest-impact channels. Repurpose existing content only.

## Notes & Lessons Learned

- v1.0 - initial version
- Tip: Run sections 1-2 with PLM. Launch narrative must be grounded in business strategy.
- What we are NOT saying is the most important discipline exercise in this prompt.
